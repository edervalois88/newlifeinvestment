# Editorial Guide - UAE-USA Corporate Positioning

## Purpose
This guide defines writing standards for a consistent institutional tone across the website in Spanish, English, and Arabic.

## Positioning
- Core axis: business expansion and investment structuring between UAE and USA.
- Brand role: strategic advisory partner, not a relocation-only service.
- Voice: precise, executive, and compliance-aware.

## Tone Rules
- Prefer: "structure", "governance", "compliance", "execution", "operating model", "roadmap".
- Avoid: exaggerated promotional phrases and absolute claims.
- Keep promises measurable and conditional (e.g., "typically", "subject to jurisdiction").

## Language Standards
- Spanish:
  - Use "EAU" or "Emiratos Arabes Unidos" consistently.
  - Use formal register: "asesoria", "estructura legal", "cumplimiento".
- English:
  - Use "UAE-USA" as standard corridor naming.
  - Prefer "strategic advisory" over purely promotional language.
- Arabic:
  - Keep formal MSA.
  - Preserve institutional wording for legal/operational contexts.

## Section Guidance
- Hero:
  - One clear strategic promise.
  - CTA should be professional (assessment, meeting, advisory).
- Services:
  - Focus on scope, method, and outcomes.
  - Avoid lifestyle-first framing.
- FAQ:
  - Prioritize legal structure, timelines, and operational setup.
  - Use qualified timeframes with dependency disclaimers.

## Approved Terms
- "UAE-USA expansion"
- "cross-border execution"
- "legal and regulatory alignment"
- "operational readiness"
- "strategic implementation"

## Terms to Avoid
- "division Mexico"
- Country-specific branding not aligned with UAE-USA positioning
- Unqualified guarantees ("fastest", "guaranteed", "zero risk")

## QA Checklist Before Publish
- No legacy geography references outside UAE-USA context.
- Tone is institutional in all three locales.
- CTA language is business-grade and action-oriented.
- Time/cost claims include context or conditions.
